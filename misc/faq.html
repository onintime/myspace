MySpace >> Help with Frequently Asked Questions
  1. Is MySpace free?
    Yes! Every feature and function you currently see on the site is FREE. MySpace is supported solely by advertising (the banners you see at the top of every page). In the future, MySpace may add paid Premium Services, but all the features and functions you have currently been enjoying on the MySpace site will always remain FREE!

  2. How do I add color, graphics, & sound to my Profile page?
    Adding color, graphics, and sound to your profile page is easy and requires only a basic knowledge of HTML (the programming language used to create web pages on the Internet). Simply go to "Edit Profile" and enter the desired HTML coding where appropriate.

    If you do not know HTML, you can reach out and make a new friend by asking someone who has color, graphics, and/or sound on their Profile page how they did it. People on MySpace are friendly and always willing to help, so just ask! This is a great way to meet new people!

  3. Why can't I upload nude pictures to my Profile?
     Well... you can, but we ask that you don't. MySpace is proud to operate as a FREE site to its membership, creating a great online community and providing a rich feature set for those within the community to interact with one and other. However, it is NOT free to develop and maintain MySpace, and therefore we rely on advertising dollars to support our efforts. The majority of mainstream advertisers refuse to advertise on websites with nudity, and simply put... no advertisers, no MySpace. So, if you enjoy the website and being part of this great community, please do not upload any nude pictures to MySpace, and if you have already uploaded nude images, please remove them. We thank you for your understanding and support.

  4. How can I change my name, e-mail address, age, and/or password?
     Name, e-mail address, age, and password may all be found under "Account Settings." Start by clicking on "Home" in the top navigation menu from any MySpace web page. Once on your personal home page, click on "Account Settings," appearing in the upper left portion of the page, next to your picture. You will be taken to your "Account Settings" page where you may edit any of your current settings for these items.

  5. How can I change and/or add to the information on my Profile?
     You may change and/or add to the information on your Profile through "Edit Profile." Start by clicking on "Home" in the top navigation menu from any MySpace web page. Once on your personal home page, click on "Edit Profile," appearing in the upper left portion of the page, next to your picture. You will be taken to your "Edit Profile" page where you may edit or add information from the following categories: city, state, zip, ethnicity, body type, reason you're on MySpace, who you are looking for, your headline, about you, interests, background, lifestyle, schools you've attended, companies you've worked at, and more!

  6. Why am I getting "Friend Requests" from people I don't know?
     MySpace is a community of very friendly individuals. When people on MySpace see your Profile, they may wish to add     you to their personal circle of friends. When this occurs, you will receive a request to be added to their personal list of friends. You may either approve or deny this request, it's completely up to you. Some people enjoy meeting new people and "collecting" friends, while others simply wish to keep their circle of friends limited to people they actually know. There is no right or wrong, and it is not considered rude to deny a friend request. The choice is always yours.

  7. How can I delete Comments on my Profile?
    To remove a comment from the "My Friends' Comments" section of your Profile, start by clicking on "Home" in the top navigation menu from any MySpace web page. Once on your personal home page, scroll down to "My Friends' Comments" and click on "Delete Comment" next to the comment you would like to remove. You may only delete one comment at a time.

  8. How can I delete friends from my Friends List on my Profile?
    To remove someone from your Friends List, start by clicking on "Home" in the top navigation menu from any MySpace web page. Once on your personal home page, scroll down to "My Friend Space" and click on "Edit Friends." You will then see a listing of every single person currently on your Friend List. Next to each name, you will see "Delete Friend." By clicking "Delete Friend," that person will be removed from your Friend List. You may only delete one person at a time.

  9. When I post a Bulletin, can everyone on MySpace see it?
     No. When you post a Bulletin, it is only seen by your direct group of friends... those that appear in your "My Friends Space." It is not seen by your extended network of friends (i.e., your friends' friends), nor the rest of the MySpace community.

  10. What is "Send a Smile"? What is "Request Review"?
     "Send a Smile" and "Request Review" are features that have been disabled (i.e., they no longer work!), and will soon be removed from MySpace completely.

  11. Why doesn't all the information I enter show up on my Profile?
     Don't worry, it will! At MySpace we are literally working around the clock to continuously add features and functionality to the website. Rest assured, if there is a place for you to enter and edit information, you will soon see that information displayed on your Profile page! We appreciate your patience, as we work feverishly to implement everything!

  12. Can I communicate with people that are not in my personal friends list?
     Yes! You can send an e-mail through the MySpace system to anyone you see on MySpace.     From the Profile page of the person you would like to communicate with, simply click on "Send an E-Mail," found under "Contacting," in the section just below the person's picture. You will then have an opportunity to create a subject line for the e-mail, as well as the main content. When the person receives your e-mail, they will see your Profile picture and a direct link to view your Profile.

  13. Can I add anyone as my friend under "My Friends Space"?
     There are 2 ways to add people to your personal list of friends found under "My Friends Space." First, you can "Invite" new people to join MySpace. If they sign-up through the Invite link you send them, they will automatically be added to your "My Friends Space" list.

    Second, you can add anyone whose Profile you see on MySpace to your     personal list of friends. From the Profile page of the person you would like to add to your personal list of friends, simply click on "Add to Friends," found under "Contacting," in the section just below the person's picture. You may add as many people to your personal friends list as you'd like (some members have THOUSANDS of people on their personal list of friends!). Keep in mind, when adding existing MySpace members to your personal list of friends through the "Add to Friends" link, the person you are attempting to add will be able to approve or deny your request to be added to your personal list of friends.

  14. What are MySpace Groups?
     MySpace Groups is an exciting way for your club or organization to network, stay informed, and promote your Group to others for FREE!     Anyone is welcome to create a MySpace Group... whether you are an official club or organization, or simply a bunch of friends that likes to hang out together. Conveniently communicate with each other, post upcoming events, invite others to join, and even promote your group's official (or unofficial!) website! MySpace Groups is the most fun you can have for FREE!

  15. How can I help MySpace?
     You can help MySpace 3 ways: Promote, Promote, Promote! MySpace is all about friends and community, and the larger that community becomes, the deeper and richer the experience will be for everyone. It's a fact, most people find new friends, dates, jobs, activity partners, advice, referrals, and just about everything else through friends. Therefore it just makes sense that the more you promote MySpace, the wider and more diverse of a personal network you will have access to. You benefit, and the entire MySpace community benefits. It's truly a win-win situation! So get out there and start promoting! (Need help or suggestions for promoting MySpace? Click here to email us for tips that work!) Note: Promoting MySpace on your own is not a paid position unless you are specifically hired by MySpace to do so.
